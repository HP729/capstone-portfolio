Overall Capstone Experience

Learning About Motivation

At the beginning of the capstone project, my motivation was strongly connected to the idea of building something useful for myself. I chose to create an adaptive task planner because I struggled with time management and wanted to develop a tool that would be genuinely useful to me. I also wanted a project that challenged me to learn new technologies and allowed me to build something functional from start to finish. This project gave me an opportunity to combine both learning and productivity into a single experience.

I also know many people who experience similar struggles with time and task management. This gave me further motivation to create the application, since it could potentially help others as well. Being able to discuss the project with classmates and people close to me made the experience more meaningful and reinforced the idea that the application had practical value.

However, as I worked on my project, I realized that motivation was not enough to maintain consistent progress. I faced challenges along the way, such as dealing with technical issues or having to spend my time on my other classes. During these periods, I noticed that my motivation would fade, especially when progress slowed or obstacles appeared. Through this experience, I learned that relying on motivation is not sustainable. Instead, consistent effort and discipline were more important in helping me continue making progress, even when I did not feel motivated.

Adapting to Unexpected Challenges

At the beginning of the project, I created a design specification document to organize my ideas and plan how the application would be developed. I outlined the features I wanted to implement and identified the technologies I expected to use. Creating this plan made me feel more prepared and gave me the sense that I would have work predetermined for me every week.

As I made progress, I realized that things do not always go as planned. I had to learn to be adaptable. Some technologies required more time to learn than I expected, and certain features were more complex to implement than I expected. I learned through my work and meetings with experts, stakeholders, and my instructor that certain technologies were not the best choice or features needed to be changed.

Through this experience, I learned that although planning is helpful, learning to be flexible is just as important when working on a large project. Even with a clear plan, unexpected challenges can occur during development. Learning to adjust my plans and continue making progress was an important skill that I developed during my capstone project.

Learning to Learn Independently

Throughout my college career, I have been learning by attending lectures and participating in assignments. However, during my capstone project, I had to learn how to teach myself new concepts independently. Instead of following a curriculum, I had to decide what information was important and how to approach learning it. At times, I felt as though I wasn't making enough progress. I was unsure if I was doing things correctly.

Instead of trying to learn everything at once, I found it helpful to figure out what I wanted to do and search for how it is accomplished. This allowed me to build my knowledge gradually. I learned through practice rather than fully understanding everything at once. Over time, I felt more comfortable working on new technologies.

This experience taught me that learning is an active process that requires patience and persistence. I realized that I am able to learn new subjects without relying on a structured course. It gave me more confidence in my ability to grow and approach new subjects. This is a skill that is going to be useful for my future career as a software engineer, as technology is always advancing.

Learning to Communicate and Seek Feedback

During my capstone project, part of my experience involved regularly discussing my progress with my instructor, stakeholders, and an expert in the technology I was using. These interactions allowed me to share my ideas, receive feedback, and better understand how others viewed my project. However, this was my first experience in having to share my technical ability with others through meetings.

At first, I was not sure what the benefits of these meetings would be. I thought that my work had to be perfect before sharing it with others. However, by sharing my work and receiving feedback, I learned the benefit of collaboration and felt more comfortable sharing my work. I began to see feedback as a helpful part of the development process rather than criticism. This shifted my way of thinking. I realized that it's acceptable to show in-progress work or work that I am not sure about.

Through this experience, I learned that communication and feedback are essential skills in software development. I need to be able to explain my ideas throughout the phases of my work and be open to feedback in order to improve my work. This is a skill that can be transferred into my future career, as collaboration and receiving feedback are something that would be required to create a polished product.

Conclusion

I have learned from my capstone experience that software development is an ongoing process. Completing my current version of the application is not the end of it. It provides a foundation that I can continue to build on and improve over time. Software development is something that is always evolving. Working with stakeholders and managers gave me a sense of purpose, as their feedback helped guide my decisions and reminded me for whom I was building the application.

I have gained some experience in understanding and building a product based on stakeholder requirements. I learned that I need to rely on consistent effort, adaptability, and independent learning in order to make progress. Even though I sometimes felt unsure about my work or faced challenges, working through these helped me become more confident in my abilities. This experience showed me that being uncertain is normal and part of the learning process. Working through these challenges is what led to my growth.
